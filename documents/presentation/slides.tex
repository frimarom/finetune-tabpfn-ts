\begin{frame}
\frametitle{Abstract}
TabPFN performes well on time series data with the right encoding (see TabPFN-TS paper)

We evaluated three methods to presumably enhance its performance on such data

\begin{itemize}
\item Finetuning on one dataset
\item Finetuning on multiple datasets
\item Continued Pretraining with synthetic data from a prior developed
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}
\frametitle{Abstract}
Single Finetuning is especially dependent on dataset characteristics

Multi Finetuning additionally showed good generalization results

Continued Pretraining did not generalize as expected and was the worst of the three methods

All methods showed promising but not decisive results
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}
    \frametitle{Time series}
    Time Series Definition:

    $X=\{x_1, x_2, …, x_n\} \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times n}$ where $n$ is the number of temporal observations and $d$ the number of observed values at time point $t$

    Observation $x_t$ Definition:
    $x_t = t_t + z_t + s_t + \epsilon_t$ \textit{or} $x_t = t_t \cdot z_t \cdot s_t \cdot \epsilon_t$

    $t_t$: trend component

    $z_t$: cyclic component

    $s_t$: season component

    $\epsilon_t$: noise component
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}
\frametitle{PFN - Prior Fitted Networks}
    - transformer based architecture

    - trains on artificial tasks generated by a prior

    - approximates posterior predictive distribution $p(y | x, D) = \int_{t} p(y|x, t)p(t, D)$

    - prior is a generative process used to produce large amounts of training data that imitates real-world data
\end{frame}
