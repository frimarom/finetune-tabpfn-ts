\section{Motivation}
\label{sec:motivation}
Since the release of ChatGPT in 2022 and the surge of interest in artificial intelligence, machine learning and deep learning have become more accessible to the public \citep{singla2025stateai}.
Large Language Models are now known as being a gateway to machine learning and artificial intelligence.
But as the name suggests, LLMs primarily excel on textual data.
However, what about tabular data?
Whether it is patient data in healthcare, sales data in finance, or any other data stored in an Excel-like schema, tabular data is one of the most common data formats used at scale \citep{van2024tabular}.
This kind of data has received significant attention over the years, and traditional machine learning approaches like gradient boosting decision trees and random forests have frequently shown better performance than deep learning models when working with tabular data.
\newline
But with the release of TabPFN, a transformer model pre-trained to solve artificially generated classification tasks from a synthetic prior, \citet{hollmann2022tabpfn} set a new performance standard for tabular inference tasks.
TabPFN achieves state-of-the-art performance on tabular classification, regression, and inference tasks.
Notably, it requires only a single forward pass.
%\\
%Early 2025 TabPFN-TS was released.
%A method that maps time series data onto tabular data to achieve SOTA forecasting results with the already pre-trained TabPFN model.

There are two main ways to presumably enhance the performance of this specific model.
One is finetuning a pre-trained model, and the other is continued pre-training of a pre-trained model.
The key difference between those two lies in the data the model is trained on.
By only using real-world datasets from a specific domain, like healthcare or finance, with finetuning, one trains a pre-trained model to enhance its accuracy in this domain.
In continued pre-training, a specific synthetic prior is used to generate synthetic classification, regression, or inference tasks for the domain to further train and enhance the model.

When it comes to physics, finance, meteorology, or any domain where predicting the future is crucial, time series forecasting models are indispensable.
A time series forecast is a forecast for the development of a system based on the current and prior observations of it, e.g., predicting the weather based on past observations and historical data from the same date in previous years.
It utilizes the temporal dependencies and intervariable correlations within the observations \citep{wang2024deep}.
\newline
There are many different approaches used in forecasting, including Multi-Layer Perceptron, Recurrent Neural Networks, and Graph Neural Networks \citep{wang2024deep}.
In this thesis, we will focus on transformer-based time series forecasting, as they have shown great modeling ability for long-range dependencies and interactions in sequential data and thus are appealing to time series modeling.
\newline
Although TabPFN for time series (TabPFN-TS) already achieves top rank results in time series forecasting, the effect of finetuning and continued pretraining on TabPFN with time series data remains largely unexplored.
\section{Exposé}
\label{sec:expose}
As \citet{hoo2025tables} showed, TabPFN can work with time series as well, although it has not been trained with time series data during pre-training, treating time series forecasting as a tabular regression problem.
The good performance on time-series data has two main causes: preprocessing the timestamp feature into automatic seasonal features, calendar features, and running index, and using a pretrained model with strong zero-shot performance on regression tasks. \newline
This thesis focuses on finetuning and continuing pretraining on TabPFN with time series data.
We will address the concept of the aforementioned methods and examine whether these methods lead to performance boosts in time series forecasting methods.
\newline
The questions we will address are:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Can we improve the performance of TabPFN-TS by finetuning on downstream tasks? (See \autoref{subsec:singlefinetuning})
    \item Can we further improve performance by finetuning on multiple datasets similar to RealTabPFN \citep{Hollmann_2025}? (See \autoref{subsec:multifinetuning})
    \item Can we continue pre-training TabPFN for time series with a prior that generates time series data (and does it improve performance)? (See \autoref{chap:continuedpretraining})
    \item Which one of the following models performs best on various high-scale benchmarks: TabPFN-TS, finetuned TabPFN-TS, or continued pre-trained TabPFN-TS? (See \autoref{chap:conclusion})
\end{enumerate}
Preliminary findings suggest that finetuning performance depends strongly on dataset characteristics, while continued pretraining does not yield the expected improvements (see Chapters \ref{chap:finetuning} and \ref{chap:continuedpretraining}).
\newline
The main contributions of this thesis are:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item We introduce a time series prior for continued pre-training of TabPFN-TS, enabling the investigation of whether synthetic time series priors can improve forecasting performance.
    \item We provide, to the best of our knowledge, the first study exploring finetuning of TabPFN for time series forecasting.
    \item We conduct a large-scale empirical comparison of finetuning and continued pre-training for TabPFN-TS across multiple benchmark datasets.
\end{enumerate}
Throughout the thesis, we will use datasets, their splits, and an evaluation pipeline of the GIFT-EVAL benchmark as well as datasets from the autogluon/chronos-datasets repository \citep{aksu2024giftevalbenchmarkgeneraltime,ansari2024chronos} .
We base our implementation on the repository by Lennart Purucker \footnote{\url{https://github.com/LennartPurucker/finetune_tabpfn_v2}}, which we adapted to time series data.
To examine the potential improvements, we use the GIFT-EVAL evaluation code to calculate different types of errors before and after finetuning.
\newline
For continued pretraining, we use the prior and its implementation from \citet{dooley2023forecastpfn} instead of real-world data.
